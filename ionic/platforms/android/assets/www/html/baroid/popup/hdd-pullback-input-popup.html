About bore length

The length of the bore and the hole's diameter drastically effect your estimated fluid volume. Here, the calculator is asking you to provide the length of the bore in linear feet

Give a best-guess estimate if the total is unknown. The estimated fluid volume is a good thing to know even before a job officially starts: How much total fluid volume are you going to have to contend with over the drill shot's life, for example?


Try it out!

Change the bore length and observe its effect on the estimated fluid volume.

About diameter

Providing the diameter of the hole in inches (an approximation) helps calculate two things:

  • Estimated funnel viscosity

  • and Estimated fluid volume.


Try it out!

Keep an eye on these two values and how they change with different hole diameters you give.

The length of the drill pipe (given in feet) factors into the recommended pullback speed you will receive.

About pump rating

No pump will supply it's rated output, especially a pump supplying drilling mud! Yet, here it is asking for the pump's output rating in gallons per minute as rated by the manufacturer. The actual pump output will be calculated for you.
